Engineering Fountain and Mall

Design and Construction

The Purdue Mall Water Sculpture, also known as the Engineering Fountain, is a remarkable creation by Robert Youngman, gifted by the class of 1939. Positioned centrally on the Purdue Mall, this 38-foot tall fountain is constructed from 228 tons of concrete and features four vertical parabolic structures. A large jet of water, which sprays upwards through the center, is complemented by a recent addition—a mirrored steel cylinder that enhances safety and aesthetics.

Cultural Significance and Traditions

The Engineering Fountain has become an iconic symbol at Purdue University, often acting as the hub of student activity. It is notably used by students for the tradition of fountain runs, which occur on warm days or to celebrate graduation. Originally, the fountain had an open jet, but in 2001, at Youngman's suggestion, a mirrored cylinder was installed to prevent injuries during these spirited activities. Despite this safety enhancement, the tradition continues unabated.

Innovative Lighting and Operation

The fountain not only serves as a daytime attraction but also features a sophisticated lighting system designed by Jones & Phillips Associates, Inc. This system includes a twenty-minute lighting program that is synchronized with the water show, creating mesmerizing effects during nighttime displays. The fountain operates from the second week of April through October, weather permitting, making it a seasonal highlight on campus.
