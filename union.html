Memorial Union

Historical Significance

The Purdue Memorial Union was inaugurated in 1924 as a tribute to Purdue students who participated and lost their lives in World War I. The building's funding began with the Class of 1912 and was further fueled by the war's impact on the Purdue community. Originally conceived as a student union, it was designed to serve as a central gathering place for students, staff, and visitors, enhancing community interaction and memorializing fallen soldiers.

Facilities and Features

The PMU is a comprehensive facility that encompasses several restaurants, lounges, and student organization offices. Additionally, it houses unique amenities such as a bowling alley and a full-service hotel, which were added over the years to increase its utility for the Purdue community. The Union's architecture and facilities have been developed to support a wide range of social, recreational, and administrative activities, making it a central hub on campus.

Cultural and Commemorative Hub

Over the years, the Purdue Memorial Union has evolved to become more than just a student union; it stands as a cultural and commemorative hub. The Great Hall, located on the main floor, serves as a memorial space honoring Purdue alumni involved in various military conflicts. The Union also supports vibrant student life through the Purdue Student Union Board (PSUB), which organizes programs and events for the university community, highlighting its role as a dynamic center for student engagement and community building.
