Slayter Center of Performing Arts

Origins and Architecture

The Slayter Center of Performing Arts, an iconic outdoor concert bandshell, was completed in 1964 and officially dedicated on May 1, 1965. Located on the main campus of Purdue University, the center was generously gifted by Games Slayter and his wife Marie. Designed by architect Joseph Baker, the Slayter Center draws architectural inspiration from Stonehenge, featuring a 200-ton concrete roof suspended by stainless steel cables from a steel tripod. This structure provides a unique visual and acoustic experience for attendees.

Facilities and Usage

Slayter Center includes a natural amphitheater on Slayter Hill, which can accommodate up to 20,000 spectators. The stage is designed to seat an orchestra of over a hundred musicians and is equipped with extensive support facilities including rehearsal rooms, dressing rooms, and storage areas. It serves as the home for the Purdue Jazz Bands and hosts a variety of performances and community events throughout the year.

Events and Community Engagement

Historically, Slayter Center was the venue for the "Thrill on the Hill" pep rally before home football games, featuring performances by the Purdue All-American Marching Band. Although the pep rally location shifted to Mackey Arena in 2013, the Slayter Center resumed hosting the post-game concert event in 2018. Additionally, the venue supports student and community engagement by hosting movie nights on a large, inflatable screen and offering free concerts. In the winter, it becomes a popular sledding spot for students, further embedding it in Purdue's campus life.
